Research Engine · System Architecture

The governed research pipeline

From a plain-language ask to a defensible insight.

Built for UX and product researchers who run the same arc on every study, then lose hours to the setup around each step. Plan, interview, synthesize, report; Research Engine does the mechanical heavy lifting at every stage. What it never does is decide. You stay the one who judges what a finding means, and the framework is reusable, so every study starts from the same rigor instead of a blank page.

Quality gate

ux-research-judge

A separate subagent, uncontaminated by the drafting context. Scores against rubrics/.

  • Per-criterion PASS / WARN / FAIL + ranked fixes
  • Bias & leading-language screen
  • Quote fidelity & participant-breadth honesty
  • Evidence proportionality

Wraps everything

Governance & guardrails

governance/ + a PreToolUse hook.

  • PII → deliverables use P1..Pn, raw data stays gitignored
  • Approval gate (GATE_MODE: warn, bypassable)
  • Anti-fabrication rules
  • No external sending without confirmation

Proven, not assumed

Regression guard

evals/, meta-evaluating the judge evals/run-evals.sh

Synthetic fixtures with planted defects (a leading discussion guide, an over-claimed synthesis) plus clean counterparts. The harness spawns the judge and asserts it flags the bad and passes the good, so tuning a rubric can't silently break the gate.

The value isn't in generating faster. It's in evaluating better. AI drafts and organizes. Human judgment is the product.
